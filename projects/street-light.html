Smart Street Light System

Overview

This project focuses on designing an intelligent street lighting system where lights automatically turn ON only when a vehicle or object is detected, helping reduce energy consumption and improve efficiency.

Problem Statement

Traditional street lights remain ON throughout the night regardless of traffic, leading to unnecessary power consumption. This project aims to address that inefficiency using sensing and control logic.

Solution

Motion sensors are used to detect the presence of a vehicle on the road. When a vehicle is detected, nearby street lights are activated, and they automatically turn OFF once the vehicle moves away.

Technologies Used

  • Embedded Systems
  • Microcontroller (Arduino / ESP)
  • Motion Sensors
  • IoT Concepts

System Architecture

The system consists of multiple ESP32-based nodes equipped with sensors to detect vehicle movement. Sensor data is communicated between nodes over HTTP, enabling coordinated control of street lights along the road.

Why This Project Matters

This project solves a real-world social problem by reducing power wastage and promoting sustainable infrastructure. It also sparked my deeper interest in embedded systems and intelligent automation.

Future Improvements

Integration with wireless communication, centralized monitoring, and intelligent decision-making can further enhance scalability and efficiency.
